## Chicken

### Dry-Rub Chicken Drumsticks

**Description:**
Oven-baked chicken drumsticks coated in a Kashmiri-style dry spice rub with chili, fennel, ginger, garlic, cinnamon, and cardamom. Best served with plain yogurt as a cooling dip.

**Time:** 55 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 8 chicken drumsticks, skin on
* 3 tbsp olive oil
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp Indian chili powder
* 1 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp ginger powder
* 1/4 tsp garlic powder
* 1/4 tsp black pepper
* 1/2 tsp cinnamon powder
* 1/4 tsp cardamom powder

**Steps**

1. Pat the drumsticks dry with paper towels.
2. Make shallow slashes in the drumsticks so the spices can coat them better.
3. Add the drumsticks and olive oil to a large bowl.
4. Mix all the spices separately to make the dry rub.
5. Add the spice mix to the chicken and rub it evenly over each drumstick.
6. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
7. Place the drumsticks on a baking tray or rack.
8. Bake for about 45 minutes, or until cooked through and lightly browned.
9. Let the drumsticks rest for 5 minutes before serving.

**Notes**

* Plain yogurt works well as a dip.
* Since this is a dry rub, the spices can be increased slightly, except for the salt.

### Chicken with Onion

**Description:**
A simple Kashmiri-style chicken dish cooked with lots of onion, fennel, chili, turmeric, ginger, garlic, and cardamom. The onions slowly soften into a flavorful base for the chicken.

**Time:** 45 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 1 lb chicken breast
* 1 lb yellow onion, chopped
* 3 tbsp canola oil
* 2 garlic cloves
* 1/4 tsp ginger powder
* 1/2 tsp fennel powder
* 1/3 tsp Indian chili powder
* 3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
* 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
* Salt, to taste
* 200–240 ml water

**Steps**

1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
2. Fry the chicken breast for about 4 minutes on each side, then remove it from the pan.
3. Add the chopped onions to the same oil.
4. Add about half of the spice mix, except the salt, along with a tiny splash of water.
5. Fry the onions until they turn light to medium brown.
6. Add the chicken back to the pan, season with salt, and fry for another 5 minutes.
7. Transfer everything to a pot.
8. Add 200–240 ml water and the remaining spice mix. Stir well.
9. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30 minutes.
10. For the last 5–10 minutes, remove the lid to let some water evaporate and thicken the curry.

**Notes**

* Works well with rice, chapati, or tortillas.
* You can adjust the water depending on how thick you want the gravy.

### Chicken with Spinach

**Description:**
A comforting chicken and spinach curry inspired by home-style Kashmiri cooking. The spinach is cooked down until soft, then simmered with chicken and warm spices.

**Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
**Serves:** 2
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 250 g spinach
* 250 g boneless chicken
* Mustard oil, as needed
* 3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp Indian chili powder
* 1/2 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
* 1/4 tsp ginger powder
* 1/8 tsp clove powder or 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 1/2 tsp garlic powder
* 3/4 tsp salt
* 240 ml water

**Steps**

1. Boil the spinach for about 10 minutes, then drain it.
2. Heat mustard oil in a pan until it starts sizzling.
3. Add the chicken and fry until lightly golden.
4. Remove the chicken from the pan.
5. Add the drained spinach to the same pan.
6. Fry the spinach thoroughly until it breaks down and becomes soft, about 10–15 minutes.
7. Add salt to the spinach.
8. Add the chicken back to the pan along with the remaining spice mix.
9. Fry everything together for a few more minutes.
10. Transfer to a pot, add 240 ml water, and bring to a boil.
11. Lower the heat and simmer for about 30 minutes.

**Notes**

* Best eaten with rice, chapati, flour tortillas, or fajitas.
* Bone-in chicken can also be used.
* A 1:1 ratio of chicken to spinach works well.
* Adjust water and spices depending on the amount of chicken and spinach.

### Marinated Chicken Drumsticks

**Description:**
Chicken drumsticks marinated in yogurt, lime, olive oil, and spices, then baked until tender and flavorful. The cuts in the chicken help the marinade soak in properly.

**Time:** 4 hours 45 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 4/5

**Ingredients**

* 8 chicken drumsticks
* Juice of 1/2 lime
* 125 ml plain yogurt
* 2 tbsp olive oil
* 2 1/4 tsp Indian chili powder
* 1 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp ginger powder
* 1/2 tsp garlic powder
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 1 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp turmeric powder

**Steps**

1. Remove the skin from the drumsticks.
2. Mix the spices together in a bowl.
3. Add the yogurt, olive oil, and lime juice to the spice mix.
4. Whisk until the marinade is smooth and evenly combined.
5. Pat the drumsticks dry and make deep cuts in them.
6. Add the drumsticks to the marinade and coat them thoroughly, making sure the marinade gets into the cuts.
7. Refrigerate for 4–12 hours.
8. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
9. Place the drumsticks on a baking tray, keeping them slightly apart.
10. Bake for 40–45 minutes.
11. Let them rest for 5 minutes before serving.

**Notes**

* Good with yogurt or hot sauce.
* A longer marinade time gives better flavor.

### Marinated Chicken Leg Quarters

**Description:**
Large chicken leg quarters marinated in yogurt, lime, spices, and Worcestershire sauce, then baked until juicy and browned. This is a stronger, richer version of the marinated drumstick recipe.

**Time:** 4 hours 45 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 2 large chicken leg quarters
* Juice of 1/2 lime
* 125 ml plain yogurt
* 2 tbsp olive oil
* 2 1/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1 1/2 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp ginger powder
* 1/2 tsp garlic powder
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
* 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

**Steps**

1. Divide the chicken leg quarters into smaller pieces.
2. Remove the skin, or partially remove it if you want some crispy skin.
3. Add the yogurt, lime juice, olive oil, Worcestershire sauce, and spices to a bowl.
4. Mix everything together to make the marinade.
5. Add the chicken and coat it thoroughly.
6. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
7. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
8. Bake for 40–45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
9. Let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

**Notes**

* Serve with plain yogurt.
* Keeping some skin on can improve texture.

### Tomato Chicken

**Description:**
A home-style tomato chicken curry made with chicken thighs, onion, garlic, and tomatoes simmered into a rich spiced gravy. Best served with rice.

**Time:** 1 hour 20 minutes
**Serves:** 2–3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 1 lb chicken thighs
* 1 lb tomatoes, diced
* 1/2 medium yellow onion, chopped
* 3 garlic cloves
* 3 tbsp canola oil
* 1 1/2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp Indian chili powder
* 3/4 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp ginger powder
* 1 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
* 1/2 tsp garam masala

**Steps**

1. Chop the onion.
2. Dice the tomatoes and garlic.
3. Heat the canola oil over medium-high heat.
4. Add the chopped onion and fry until lightly golden.
5. Add the chicken thighs and sear for about 3 minutes on each side.
6. Mix the spices together in a small bowl.
7. Remove the chicken from the pan.
8. Add the tomatoes and half of the spice mix.
9. Fry until the tomatoes start to break down.
10. Add the chicken back to the pot along with the remaining spice mix.
11. Cover and simmer on low heat for about 50 minutes.
12. For the last 15 minutes, remove the lid to thicken the gravy.
13. Stir occasionally while cooking.

**Notes**

* Best eaten with rice.
* The gravy should become thick and tomato-rich by the end.

## Lamb

### Ground Lamb with Tomato Curry

**Description:**
Ground lamb cooked with tomatoes, onion, garlic, bell pepper, and Kashmiri-style spices. The tomatoes cook down into a thick curry base that pairs well with rice or flatbread.

**Time:** 1 hour 5 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 1 lb ground lamb
* 3 tomatoes, about 400–450 g, diced
* 1 bell pepper, chopped
* 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
* 2 garlic cloves
* 3 tbsp canola oil
* 3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp Indian chili powder
* 3/4 tsp fennel powder
* 1/3 tsp ginger powder
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 1/2 tsp garlic powder
* 1/2 tsp salt

**Steps**

1. Heat the oil over medium-high heat.
2. Fry the onion and garlic until browned.
3. Add the ground lamb and fry until lightly browned.
4. Add the diced tomatoes and about three-quarters of the spice mix.
5. Add the bell pepper and continue frying until the tomatoes dissolve into a paste.
6. Move the mixture to a pot and bring it to a boil.
7. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.
8. For the last 15 minutes, remove the lid and let extra water evaporate.

**Notes**

* Works well with rice, chapati, tortillas, or flatbread.
* Letting the tomatoes fully break down gives the best texture.

### Lamb Flatbread

**Description:**
A quick lamb flatbread made by pressing spiced ground lamb onto tortillas and pan-frying until crisp and browned. Simple, fast, and great with yogurt or hot sauce.

**Time:** 30 minutes
**Serves:** 1
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 1/3 lb ground lamb
* 3 small tortillas
* 1 tomato, diced
* 1 tbsp canola oil
* 1/4 tsp Indian chili powder
* 1/4 tsp fennel powder
* 1/8 tsp ginger powder
* 1/8 tsp garlic powder
* 1/8 tsp garam masala
* 1/4 tsp salt

**Steps**

1. Add the spice mix to the ground lamb and mix until evenly combined.
2. Scoop the lamb onto each tortilla and spread it into a thin layer.
3. Dice the tomato and press small pieces into the lamb layer.
4. Heat canola oil in a pan over medium heat.
5. Place the tortilla lamb-side down and fry for about 3 minutes, pressing gently.
6. Flip and fry the tortilla side for about 30 seconds.
7. Let it cool slightly before eating.

**Notes**

* Plain yogurt can be added as a spread after cooking.
* Tabasco or hot sauce also works well.

### Lamb Meatballs with Tomato

**Description:**
Tender lamb meatballs simmered in a tomato and bell pepper curry. This is a home-style dish with a rich sauce and classic Kashmiri spice profile.

**Time:** 1 hour 20 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 450 g ground lamb
* 3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
* 1/2 medium yellow onion, chopped
* 2 garlic cloves, chopped
* 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
* 3 tbsp mustard oil
* 1/4 tsp ginger powder
* 1/4 tsp garlic powder
* 1/2 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
* 3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 3/4 tsp salt

**Steps**

1. Roll the ground lamb into small meatballs. For 450 g lamb, you should get around 16–17 meatballs.
2. Chop the onion and garlic.
3. Heat mustard oil in a pan until slightly sizzling.
4. Add the onion and garlic and fry until golden.
5. Add the meatballs and fry on both sides until they change from pink to grey and become firm, about 5–6 minutes per side.
6. Finely chop the tomatoes and bell pepper, then add them to the pan.
7. Add half of the spice mix and stir everything together.
8. Fry for 5–10 minutes, then remove the meatballs.
9. Continue frying the tomatoes and bell pepper until the tomatoes dissolve.
10. Transfer everything to a pot.
11. Add the meatballs back in, along with the remaining spices.
12. Make sure the tomato mixture covers the meatballs. Add a small amount of water if needed.
13. Bring to a boil.
14. Cover, lower the heat, and simmer for 45 minutes.

**Notes**

* Best served with rice.
* Be gentle while stirring so the meatballs do not break.

### Potato Lamb Curry

**Description:**
A lamb and potato curry made with rib-cut lamb breast, tomatoes, onion, garlic, and Kashmiri spices. The lamb is simmered first, then potatoes are added so they become tender without falling apart.

**Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes
**Serves:** 3
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 1 lb split lamb breast plate
* 14 bite-sized yellow potatoes, halved
* 2 tomatoes, chopped
* 2 garlic cloves, chopped
* 1/2 large onion, chopped
* 3 tbsp canola oil
* 1 1/2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
* 3/4 tsp fennel powder
* 3/4 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 2/3 tsp ginger powder
* About 125 ml water, plus more if needed

**Steps**

1. Cut the lamb breast plate into vertical rib pieces.
2. Chop the onion, tomatoes, and garlic.
3. Mix the spices together.
4. Heat the oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
5. Fry the lamb ribs until browned, about 5 minutes.
6. Remove the lamb from the pan.
7. Add the onion, tomatoes, garlic, and spice mix to the same pan.
8. Fry until the tomatoes start breaking down.
9. Add the lamb back and fry for another 5 minutes.
10. Add about 125 ml water, or enough so the lamb is nearly submerged.
11. Cover and simmer on low-medium heat for 30 minutes.
12. Cut the small potatoes in half and add them to the curry.
13. Lower the heat and simmer for another 30 minutes with the lid closed.
14. Add a little more water only if needed.

**Notes**

* The lamb may need more simmering time if it is still chewy.
* Serve with rice.
* Roma tomatoes were used, but other tomatoes should also work.

## Vegetarian

### Tomato Curry

**Description:**
A simple tomato curry made with onions, fennel, Kashmiri chili, turmeric, garlic, and garam masala. A good vegetarian base curry that works well with rice or flatbread.

**Time:** 50 minutes
**Serves:** 2
**Rating:** 5/5

**Ingredients**

* 3–4 tomatoes on the vine, diced
* 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
* 2 tbsp canola oil
* 1/2 tsp fennel powder
* 3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/3 tsp turmeric powder
* 1/2 tsp garam masala
* 1/2 tsp garlic powder
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 60–80 ml water

**Steps**

1. Finely chop the onion.
2. Dice the tomatoes.
3. Heat the oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
4. Add the onions and fry until golden brown.
5. Add the tomatoes and continue frying until they break down.
6. Reduce the heat.
7. Add 60–80 ml water and the spice mix.
8. Stir thoroughly.
9. Cover and cook on low heat for about 30 minutes.

**Notes**

* Beefsteak tomatoes can be used.
* Avoid Roma tomatoes if possible.
* Mustard oil can be used instead of canola oil.

## Fish

### Pan-Seared Tilapia with Garlic Butter and Lime

**Description:**
A quick pan-seared tilapia recipe finished with garlic butter and lime. Light, fast, and good when you want something simple but flavorful.

**Time:** 20 minutes
**Serves:** 1
**Rating:** 4/5

**Ingredients**

* 2 tilapia fillets
* Salt, to taste
* Black pepper, to taste
* 2 tbsp olive oil
* 2 tbsp butter
* 2 garlic cloves, minced
* Juice of 1 lime
* Parsley, optional

**Steps**

1. Rinse the tilapia fillets and pat them completely dry with paper towels.
2. Season both sides generously with salt and black pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
4. Once the oil is shimmering, add the fillets.
5. Cook for 3–4 minutes per side, until a golden crust forms.
6. Reduce the heat to medium-low.
7. Add butter and minced garlic to the pan.
8. Sauté for about 1 minute, being careful not to burn the garlic.
9. Add the lime juice.
10. Spoon the garlic butter and lime sauce over the fish for about 30 seconds.
11. Remove from heat and garnish with parsley if desired.

**Notes**

* Do not overcook the tilapia; it cooks quickly.
* Patting the fish dry helps it sear better.

### Spiced Tilapia Fillets

**Description:**
Tilapia fillets rubbed with fennel, Kashmiri chili, cinnamon, salt, and black pepper, then quickly pan-fried. A simple spiced fish recipe with a Kashmiri-inspired flavor profile.

**Time:** 22 minutes
**Serves:** 1
**Rating:** 3/5

**Ingredients**

* 2 thawed tilapia fillets
* 2 tbsp canola oil
* 1/2 tsp fennel powder
* 1/2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 1/3 tsp cinnamon powder
* 1/4 tsp black pepper

**Steps**

1. Mix the spices together.
2. Pat the tilapia fillets dry with paper towels.
3. Rub the spice mix onto both sides of the fillets, pressing gently so it sticks.
4. Heat canola oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
5. Add the fillets and fry for about 3 minutes on each side.
6. Remove from the pan and serve immediately.

**Notes**

* This is a quick recipe, so keep an eye on the fish.
* The spice rub can be adjusted based on how strong you want the flavor.
